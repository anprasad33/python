In Python,
the print() function sends data to the screen,
while the input() function receives data from the user.
Together, they form the foundation of interactive terminal programming

Variable Naming RulesTo keep your code valid and clean, 
you must follow specific naming rules.Allowed: Letters, numbers, and underscores (e.g., user_1).
Forbidden: Starting with a number (e.g., 1user is invalid).Forbidden: Spaces or special characters like -, !, @.Case-Sensitive: age, Age, and AGE are three different variables.Style: Use snake_case (lowercase words separated by underscores) for readability.
